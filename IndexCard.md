# Index Card

Two slightly different variations of the same idea, keep investing simple.

[All the financial advice you'll ever need fits on a 4 x 6 index card](https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/wonk/wp/2013/09/16/this-4x6-index-card-has-all-the-financial-advice-youll-ever-need/)  

1. Save 20% of your money.
2. Pay your credit card balance in full every month.
3. Maximize tax-advantaged savings vehicles like Roth IRA, SEP, and 529 accounts.
4. Max out your 401(k) or equivalent employer contribution.
5. Buy inexpensive, well-diversified mutual funds and exchange traded funds (EFTs).
6. Pay attention to fees. Avoid actively managed funds. Buy low cost index funds instead.
7. Never buy or sell an individual security. The person of the other side of the table knows more than you.
8. Make your financial advisor commit to a fiduciary standard.
9. Promote social insurance programs to help people when things go wrong.

[Freakonomics Podcast](http://freakonomics.com/podcast/everything-always-wanted-know-money-afraid-ask-rebroadcast/)  

1. Strive to save 10 to 20% of your gross income every month.
2. Pay your credit card balance in full every month.
3. Max out your 401(k) and other tax-advantaged savings vihicles like Roth IRA, SEP, and 529 accounts.
4. Never buy or sell an individual security.
5. Buy inexpensive, well-diversified mutual funds and exchange traded funds (EFTs).
   Pay attention to fees. Avoid actively managed funds. Buy low cost index funds instead.
6. If you feel you must have one, make your financial advisor commit to the fiduciary standard.
7. Buy a home when you are financially ready, e.g., have saved the 20% down payment for a fixed rate mortgage.
8. Insurance. Make sure you're protected against a life-changing event.
9. Do what you can to support the social safety net.
10. Remember the index card.
